# Unclutter GPT — Style Guide

This file defines how to apply user-specified style preferences when 
restyling a room. Consult this file whenever the user names a style.

These guidelines layer on top of the base Room Mood (Warm vs Fresh). 
If a style modifier conflicts with the base mood, the style modifier wins.

---

## How to Use This File

1. User names a style (e.g., "I want it to feel masculine" or "make it 
   more minimalist")
2. Find the matching style below
3. Apply its palette, materials, and rules to every decision: SWAP, ADD, 
   RESTYLE, and the generated image
4. If the user's style preference is not listed here, extract its core 
   feeling (grounded? soft? bold?) and apply the closest match, then 
   adapt

## Style Modifiers

---

### Masculine

**What it means:** Structured, grounded, understated, refined.

**What it does NOT mean:** All-black, dark industrial, or harsh. 
That is a cliche. Avoid it entirely.

- **Materials:** Matte wood, stone, concrete, brushed metal, leather, 
  ceramic in neutral or earthy tones
- **Palette:** Warm grey, charcoal, olive, slate, walnut brown, off-white, 
  sand. One dark anchor is fine — never the whole room.
- **Textiles:** Structured cotton, canvas, linen in neutral tones. 
  No florals, no sheer fabrics.
- **ADD items:** Wood tray, matte ceramic container, leather catch-all, 
  stone dish, glass jar for pens
- **Avoid:** Glossy black surfaces, harsh cool greys, all-dark palettes, 
  bright plastics, anything that reads "bachelor pad cliche"
- **Reference aesthetic:** Architect's desk, Japanese minimalism, 
  upscale men's grooming brand, Kinfolk magazine

---

### Feminine

**What it means:** Warm, layered, intentional, sensory.

**What it does NOT mean:** All-pink or overly sweet. Avoid cliche.

- **Materials:** Linen, rattan, ceramic, soft wood, glass, brass
- **Palette:** Cream, blush, terracotta, dusty rose, warm white, sage, 
  soft lavender
- **Textiles:** Encouraged — throws, cushions, table runners in 
  natural fibers
- **ADD items:** Brass tray, ceramic bowl, woven basket, linen throw, 
  dried botanicals (not fresh flowers unless already present)
- **Avoid:** Hot pink, plastic-looking accessories, overly matchy 
  color sets
- **Reference aesthetic:** Curated Etsy shop, soft editorial, warm 
  neutral interiors

---

### Minimalist

**What it means:** Intentional reduction. Every item earns its place.

- **Materials:** Light wood, stone, glass, matte white ceramic
- **Palette:** White, light grey, natural wood, one quiet accent at most
- **Textiles:** One, maximum two. Folded or draped with precision.
- **Surfaces:** Maximum 2-3 items visible per surface. No exceptions.
- **ADD items:** Only if the space feels cold or unfinished without 
  something. A single ceramic bowl or one book is enough.
- **Avoid:** Collections on display, layered shelves, pattern mixing, 
  anything purely decorative
- **Reference aesthetic:** Japanese wabi-sabi, Muji, Norm Architects

---

### Maximalist / Eclectic / Bohemian

**What it means:** Layered, collected, full of personality. 
Every item has a story.

- **Materials:** Mix freely — rattan, velvet, brass, ceramic, 
  printed textiles, natural wood
- **Palette:** Warm tones mixing freely — terracotta, rust, mustard, 
  deep green, cobalt, burgundy. Intentional clashes are fine.
- **Textiles:** Encouraged and layered — rugs, throws, cushions, 
  wall hangings
- **ADD items:** Plants are appropriate here. So are candles, 
  art objects, stacked books, baskets.
- **Preserve:** Collections and meaningful items prominently. 
  This is a style where personality should dominate.
- **Avoid:** Making it look sparse. The goal is curated abundance, 
  not empty shelves.
- **Reference aesthetic:** Justina Blakeney, vintage bazaar, 
  well-traveled home

---

### Scandinavian / Nordic

**What it means:** Functional warmth. Light, clean, and human.

- **Materials:** Light wood (birch, pine, ash), white, wool, 
  natural cotton, ceramic
- **Palette:** White, off-white, soft grey, muted sage, light 
  natural wood tones. Occasional muted dusty pink or blue as accent.
- **Textiles:** Natural fibers only — wool, cotton, linen. 
  Subtle texture over pattern.
- **ADD items:** Functional objects styled as decor — a wooden 
  bowl, a ceramic pitcher, a folded wool throw
- **Avoid:** Bright saturated colors, ornate patterns, dark woods, 
  heavy furniture feel
- **Reference aesthetic:** HAY, Muuto, hygge interiors, 
  Swedish design blogs

---

### Industrial

**What it means:** Raw, functional, unapologetically structural.

- **Materials:** Exposed metal, reclaimed wood, concrete, leather, 
  matte black accents (used sparingly — one or two pieces max)
- **Palette:** Charcoal, warm grey, rust, raw wood, off-white, 
  black as an accent only
- **Textiles:** Minimal — canvas, rough linen, worn leather. 
  No soft or delicate fabrics.
- **ADD items:** Metal wire basket, leather strap organizer, 
  raw wood tray, glass and metal containers
- **Avoid:** All-black rooms, cold blue-grey palettes, 
  anything that feels overly polished or fussy
- **Reference aesthetic:** Brooklyn loft, workshop-turned-studio, 
  coffee roastery interiors

---

### Coastal / Relaxed

**What it means:** Breezy, light, easy. Not nautical kitsch.

- **Materials:** Whitewashed wood, rattan, linen, sea glass, 
  natural fiber rugs
- **Palette:** White, sand, warm grey, soft blue, sage, 
  driftwood tones
- **Textiles:** Linen and cotton encouraged — lightweight, 
  slightly relaxed, not crisp
- **ADD items:** Woven basket, simple ceramic, a single piece 
  of driftwood or stone, linen throw
- **Avoid:** Anchors, shells, anything that reads "beach souvenir 
  shop." Coastal means light and airy, not themed.
- **Reference aesthetic:** Australian beach house, 
  relaxed Mediterranean, Serena & Lily

---

### Earthy / Organic

**What it means:** Grounded, natural, textural. Brings the 
outside in without being rustic.

- **Materials:** Raw ceramics, jute, linen, unfinished wood, 
  stone, dried botanicals
- **Palette:** Terracotta, clay, warm brown, forest green, 
  oatmeal, sand
- **Textiles:** Natural and textural — jute rug, linen throw, 
  chunky knit cushion
- **ADD items:** Raw ceramic vase, stone bowl, dried stems 
  (pampas, eucalyptus), woven tray
- **Avoid:** Glossy finishes, cool greys, synthetic-looking 
  materials, anything that feels manufactured
- **Reference aesthetic:** Architectural Digest earthy homes, 
  natural dye ceramics studios, slow living aesthetic

---

## General Rules for All Styles

- Never default to a cliche. Every style has a lazy version and 
  an elevated version. Always aim for the elevated version.
- One strong palette anchor beats five scattered accent colors.
- If the user says a style but the room's bones don't support it 
  (e.g., they want minimalist but have a maximalist collection), 
  acknowledge the tension and suggest a middle path rather than 
  bulldozing their personality.
- Style modifiers affect SWAP choices, ADD choices, the generated 
  image, and the palette used in the restyled visual. All four 
  must align.
- If the user names a style and then says "but not [cliche version]," 
  note that explicitly and make sure the generated image reflects 
  the correction.
